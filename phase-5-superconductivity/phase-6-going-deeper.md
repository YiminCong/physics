# Phase 6 — Going Deeper (optional, research-level)

> Format: Definition → Demonstration → Application

A short orientation rather than full modules — pursue this only if you want the modern
research toolkit.

**Definition / scope.** Modern superconductivity research is written in **quantum many-body theory**: second quantization (Module 3.12) extended with **Green's functions** (propagators that track particles and holes), **Feynman diagrams**, and **path integrals / field theory** (the action formalism of Module 1.4 promoted to fields).

**Demonstration of the route.**
- **Coleman, *Introduction to Many-Body Physics*** — the modern, readable entry: second quantization, Green's functions, and BCS rederived in this language.
- **Fetter & Walecka, *Quantum Theory of Many-Particle Systems*** — the classic; derives superconductivity via Green's functions (the Nambu–Gor'kov formalism).
- **Altland & Simons, *Condensed Matter Field Theory*** — path integrals and the renormalization group for the frontier (including unconventional pairing).
- *(Optional QFT: Peskin & Schroeder — helpful background, not required for the condensed-matter route.)*

**Application.** This machinery is what you need to engage with current literature on cuprates, iron-based and topological superconductors, and to read arXiv cond-mat papers. It's also the shared language of the rest of modern condensed-matter physics.
