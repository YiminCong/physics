## Why these matter downstream

- **0.3** — the constant-coefficient second-order ODE *is* the time-independent Schrödinger
  equation for constant-potential regions; special functions (Hermite, Legendre, Laguerre)
  reappear as oscillator, angular-momentum, and hydrogen wavefunctions in Phase 3.
- **0.4** — complex amplitudes underlie all of quantum mechanics, and the *phase* of the
  complex order parameter ψ = |ψ|e^{iφ} is what drives supercurrents and the Josephson
  effect in Phase 5.
