# Module 4.1 — Electrons and Heat in Solids

> **Phase 4 — [Condensed Matter / Solid State](./README.md)** · Format: Definition → Demonstration → Application

---

## 1. From Drude to Sommerfeld

**Definition.** The **Drude model** treats conduction electrons as a classical gas — it gets Ohm's law right but predicts a far-too-large electronic heat capacity. The **Sommerfeld model** fixes this by treating electrons as a quantum **free Fermi gas** obeying Fermi–Dirac statistics (Module 2.5).

**Demonstration.** Because only electrons within ~kT of the Fermi energy can be thermally excited, the electronic heat capacity is **C_el = γT ∝ T** (linear, and small), with γ proportional to the density of states at the Fermi level.

## 2. Lattice Heat Capacity

**Definition.** The **Einstein** and **Debye** models quantize lattice vibrations. Debye correctly gives **C_lattice ∝ T³** at low temperature.

**Application.** Total low-T heat capacity **C = γT + βT³**: the linear term probes electrons, the cubic term probes phonons. Measuring γ gives the density of states at E_F — and the **jump in heat capacity at T_c** is one of the classic fingerprints of the superconducting transition (Phase 5).
